**Euler substitution** is used for solving integral as:
$$
\int R(x,\sqrt{ax^2+bx+c})dx
$$
where $R(x,y)$ is a rational fraction of $x,y$ and $ax^2+bx+c$ has no repeated root.



## First kind of substitution

> when $a>0$

then substitute with:
$$
\sqrt {ax^2+bx+c}=t-\sqrt{a}t\quad \text{or}\quad t+\sqrt{a}t
$$


## Second kind of substitution

> when $c<0$

then substitute with:
$$
\sqrt {ax^2+bx+c}=xt+c
$$


## Third kind of substitution

> when it can be expressed that$ax^2+bx+c=a(x-\mu)(x-\lambda)$

then substitute with:
$$
\sqrt {ax^2+bx+c}=t(x-\lambda)
$$
